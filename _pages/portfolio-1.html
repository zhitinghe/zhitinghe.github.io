MoonTide VR Experience

“I Feel Myself So Small!”: Designing and Evaluating VR Awe Experiences Based on Theories Related to Sublime

Zhiting He, Min Fan, Xinyi Guo, Yifan Zhao, Yuqiu Wang
IEEE International Symposium on Mixed and Augmented Reality (ISMAR 2024)
Paper

MoonTide
VR Experience

A Unity-based VR prototype designed to evoke awe through scale, motion, environmental contrast, and embodied interaction.

Experience Overview

MoonTide is a VR awe experience built around a shoreline scene shaped by a vast moon, rising waves, extreme light contrast, and environmental progression. The experience draws on concepts related to the sublime and translates them into scene elements that emphasize scale, instability, distance, and unfamiliarity.

The scene begins in a darker, more uncertain atmosphere, gradually intensifies through the appearance of the oversized moon and the rise of huge waves, and eventually transitions toward daylight as the moon sets and the waves move away.

Experience Progression

(a) Calm shoreline at dusk

The user first encounters a relatively calm shoreline environment that establishes spatial openness and a sense of anticipation.

(b) Vast moon emergence

The oversized moon appears as a major source of visual and spatial contrast, shifting the user’s perception of scale.

(c) Wave escalation and intensity

Large waves, sound, and dramatic lighting intensify the environment and build emotional pressure.

(d) Moonset and return of daylight

As the moon gradually sets and daylight returns, the environment shifts away from intensity toward distance and release.

Design Translation

The scene design was developed by translating high-level sublime concepts into concrete VR elements such as environmental scale, wave motion, sound intensity, lighting contrast, and temporal progression.

Key Scene Elements

Terror — power and threat

  • • Huge waves
  • • Tremendous sound
  • • A vast moon suddenly appears overhead
  • • Extreme light in contrast to the darkness of the night

Distance or modification — distance and release

  • • The huge waves are at a distance from the shore
  • • The huge waves gradually move away
  • • The vast moon eventually sets, as the sun begins to rise

Obscurity — unfamiliar scale

  • • The vast moon is much larger than what we usually see in daily life

Scene Variants

Two scene variants were developed: a visually focused awe experience, and an embodied version that added virtual body alignment and tactile cues.

VR-Sublime

A visually focused version emphasizing the moon, waves, light contrast, and environmental progression.

VR-Embodied Sublime

An embodied version that additionally incorporated virtual body alignment and tactile cues to strengthen presence and bodily engagement.

Embodied Interaction

Visuomotor Synchronization

The user’s movements are aligned with the virtual body, allowing control over virtual arms and head rotation for a 360-degree embodied view of the scene.

Visuotactile Cue

Water is sprinkled onto the user during the wave event, extending the experience beyond the visual environment and reinforcing the embodied intensity of the scene.
