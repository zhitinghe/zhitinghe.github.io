Thunderstorm
VR Awe Experience Prototype

A rapid Unreal Engine VR prototype using lightning, fire, rain, and regrowth to guide an experience from threat-driven awe to calm and renewal.

Experience Progression

(a) Shelter and Tension

The user begins inside a small stone shelter. A campfire suggests warmth and safety, while the outside environment is dark, cloudy, and uncertain.

(b) First Lightning Strike

A distant tree is struck by lightning, introducing the first visible sign of danger.

(c) Escalation

Lightning strikes again closer to the user. Fire spreads across trees and grass, increasing intensity and perceived threat.

(d) Resolution and Renewal

Rain extinguishes the fire. The sky clears, sunlight returns, and burnt ground begins to regrow with grass and flowers.

Experience Design Framework

The following framework guided how the thunderstorm prototype was structured, from defining the awe content to shaping the experience flow and virtual environment.

From Framework to Scene Design

  • Familiarize with the virtual environment — The experience begins by giving the user time to become familiar with both the shelter and the stormy outdoor environment. The outside scene is already dark, cloudy, and unclear, establishing uncertainty from the beginning.
  • Restrict the field of view / simplify non-essential elements — The user is placed inside a small stone shelter and can only see the outdoor scene through the open front doorway. Side and rear views are blocked, and only the essential scene elements are retained: the campfire, the grassland, and the trees.
  • Highlight the awe object — Lightning is introduced as the central awe-inducing phenomenon. Its power is revealed through its effects on the environment: striking trees, igniting fire, and spreading across the grass.
  • Provide attention-guiding cues — Thunder helps direct the user’s attention toward the location of the lightning strikes and strengthens the sense of pressure in the environment.
  • Create an obscure context — During the storm stages, the environment remains dark, foggy, and unstable. Heavy clouds, fog, and continuous thunder reinforce a sense of obscurity and threat.
  • Move toward a positive outcome — In the final stage, rain extinguishes the fire, the fog disappears, and sunlight returns. Burnt ground begins to regrow with grass and flowers, transforming the environment into a brighter and safer space.
  • Support a positive emotional ending — By removing threat and restoring visibility, warmth, and signs of life, the experience is designed to guide the user toward calmness and a more positive emotional state.
