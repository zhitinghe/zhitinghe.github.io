Thunderstorm
VR Awe Experience Prototype

A rapid Unreal Engine VR prototype using lightning, fire, rain, and regrowth to guide an experience from threat-driven awe to calm and renewal.

Experience Progression

(a) Shelter and Tension

The user begins inside a small stone shelter. A campfire suggests warmth and safety, while the outside environment is dark, cloudy, and uncertain.

(b) First Lightning Strike

A distant tree is struck by lightning, introducing the first visible sign of danger.

(c) Escalation

Lightning strikes again closer to the user. Fire spreads across trees and grass, increasing intensity and perceived threat.

(d) Resolution and Renewal

Rain extinguishes the fire. The sky clears, sunlight returns, and burnt ground begins to regrow with grass and flowers.

Design Focus

Awe Trigger

Lightning was chosen as the core awe-inducing force because it is widely recognizable as powerful, dangerous, and uncontrollable. Its impact is expressed through visible changes in the surrounding environment.

Emotional Arc

The experience is structured as a transition:

  • uncertainty and observation
  • threat and escalation
  • release and recovery
  • renewal and positive meaning

Environmental Framing

The stone shelter acts as a framing device. By restricting peripheral views, it focuses attention on the outdoor scene and reinforces the contrast between interior safety and exterior danger.

Spatial Setup

The environment was intentionally simplified to keep attention on the awe-related events.

Inside the shelter, only a few elements are present, with the campfire providing warmth and a sense of protection. Outside, the grassland and trees form the main stage for the unfolding thunderstorm.

The experience uses a low-agency first-person setup: the user cannot move or interact with objects, but can freely turn their head and body to observe the changing environment.

Note

This rapid prototype focuses on experience structure, event sequencing, and environmental transformation rather than visual polish.
